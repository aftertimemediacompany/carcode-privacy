CarCode — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 7, 2026

CarCode ("the app") is a car owner's assistant: it decodes OBD-II trouble codes, checks manufacturer recalls and keeps your car diary, reminders and fuel log. This policy explains what data the app handles. The short version: we do not collect, store or share any of your personal data.

Data we collect

None. The app has no accounts, no analytics, no advertising SDKs and no tracking. We do not collect personal information, device identifiers, location, or usage statistics.

Data stored on your device

Everything you enter — your vehicles (including VIN, if you choose to use it), diary entries, reminders, fuel records and settings — is stored locally on your device only. It never leaves your phone, and it is deleted when you uninstall the app.

Network requests

The app contacts public government data services directly from your device, only to fetch information you request:

  • NHTSA (api.nhtsa.gov, vpic.nhtsa.dot.gov) — to decode a VIN you enter and to look up US recall campaigns for your car's make, model and year;
  • EU Safety Gate / RAPEX — to look up EU safety alerts.

These requests include only the vehicle parameters needed for the lookup (e.g. make, model, year, or the VIN you typed). We do not attach any personal identifiers. The trouble-code database is bundled inside the app and works offline.

Notifications

If you enable reminders, notifications are scheduled locally on your device. No push-notification service receives your data.

Children

The app does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

Changes

If a future version of the app starts processing data differently, this policy will be updated before that version is released.
